Problem Statement: Medical Mesh Processing Challenge

Medical Imaging Pipeline

  1. MRI Scan: 3D volumetric data (1mm³ voxels)
  2. AI Segmentation: Identify tumor regions (BraTS labels)
  3. Surface Extraction: Marching Cubes algorithm
  4. Mesh Smoothing: Remove staircase artifacts
  5. Application: Surgical planning, volumetrics

The Critical Problem

Marching Cubes produces:

  • Jagged staircase artifacts at voxel boundaries
  • Poor triangle quality (elongated, degenerate triangles)
  • Artificial high-curvature regions at voxel corners
  • Visual noise obscuring fine anatomical details

Why Traditional Smoothing Fails

Laplacian smoothing causes 1-4% volume shrinkage - unacceptable for tumor monitoring where FDA requires <1% error!

Project Goal: Achieve <0.1% volume change while maintaining high visual quality - 10× better than the FDA requirement

Fig 1: Medical Imaging to Mesh Pipeline

Medical Imaging Requirements (RECIST 1.1)

  • Volume accuracy: <1% error
  • Preserve anatomical boundaries
  • Interactive workflows (sub-second feedback)

How the Software Processes Our Data (Per Sample)

  1. Input: 3D segmentation mask (BraTS labels in a NIfTI volume)
  2. Marching Cubes: extract a surface mesh (vertices + faces)
  3. Weights: build adjacency / cotangent weights (algorithm-dependent)
  4. Iterate: update vertex positions for $k$ steps (faces fixed)
  5. Evaluate & Export: compute metrics (before/after), visualize in Streamlit, optionally export STL/figures

Baseline: Laplacian Smoothing

Mathematical Formulation

Laplacian Operator
$$ L(\mathbf{v}_i) = \frac{1}{|N(i)|} \sum_{j \in N(i)} \mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i $$
Update Rule
$$ \mathbf{v}_i' = \mathbf{v}_i + \lambda \cdot L(\mathbf{v}_i), \quad \lambda = 0.5 $$
Algorithm 1: Laplacian Smoothing
1: Input: Mesh M, λ=0.5, iterations k
2: W ← BuildAdjacency(M) // Row-normalized
3: K ← (1-λ)I + λW
4: for i = 1 to k do
5:   V ← K · V
6: return V

State-of-the-Art: Taubin λ|μ Smoothing

Signal Processing Perspective

Key insight: Laplacian acts as low-pass filter. Solution: alternate shrink/expand steps.

Two-Step Process
$$ \mathbf{v}' = \mathbf{v} + \lambda \cdot L(\mathbf{v}) \quad \text{(shrink)} $$ $$ \mathbf{v}'' = \mathbf{v}' + \mu \cdot L(\mathbf{v}') \quad \text{(expand)} $$

Parameters: λ = 0.5, μ = -0.53 (0 < λ < -μ)

Algorithm 2: Taubin Smoothing
1: Input: Mesh M, λ=0.5, μ=-0.53, k
2: K_λ ← (1-λ)I + λW
3: K_μ ← (1-μ)I + μW
4: for i = 1 to k do
5:   V ← K_λ · V // Shrink
6:   V ← K_μ · V // Expand
7: return V

Novel Method 1 (This Work): Geodesic Heat Diffusion Smoothing

Key Concept

Use geodesic distances (along surface) instead of Euclidean for neighbor weighting.

Heat Kernel Weights
$$ K_t(x,y) = \exp\left(-\frac{d_{geo}(x,y)^2}{4t}\right) $$
Cotangent Laplacian
$$ H(\mathbf{v}_i) = \frac{1}{4A} \sum_{j \in N(i)} (\cot \alpha_{ij} + \cot \beta_{ij})(\mathbf{v}_i - \mathbf{v}_j) $$
Algorithm 3: Geodesic Heat Smoothing
1: Input: Mesh M, time t, iterations k
2: L_cot ← BuildCotangentLaplacian(M)
3: for each edge (i,j) do
4:   d_geo[i,j] ← ApproxGeodesic(L_cot, i, j)
5:   W[i,j] ← exp(-d_geo²/4t)
6: for iter = 1 to k do
7:   V ← Normalize(W) · V
8: return V

Novel Method 2 (This Work): Information-Theoretic Smoothing

Key Idea

Use Shannon entropy of local curvature to separate noise (high entropy) from structured features (low entropy).

Entropy-Based Weight
$$ H(v) = -\sum_{i=1}^{n} p(\kappa_i) \log p(\kappa_i) $$ $$ w(v) = \sigma(H(v) - \tau) $$

Intuition

  • High entropy: random curvature/noise → smooth more
  • Low entropy: coherent features → preserve
  • Adaptive: different smoothing strength per vertex
Algorithm 4: Info-Theoretic Smoothing
1: Input: Mesh M, threshold τ, iterations k
2: κ ← EstimateCurvature(M)
3: for vertex v do H[v] ← Entropy(κ[N(v)])
4: for vertex v do w[v] ← sigmoid(H[v] - τ)
5: for iter = 1..k do V ← SmoothWeighted(V, w)
6: return V

Novel Method 3 (This Work): Anisotropic Tensor Smoothing

Key Idea

Build a directional diffusion tensor per vertex so smoothing happens preferentially along stable directions, not across sharp features.

Diffusion Tensor
$$ D = \lambda_\parallel\, e_1 e_1^T + \lambda_\perp\, e_2 e_2^T $$

Intuition

  • Estimate principal curvature directions $(e_1, e_2)$
  • Diffuse strongly in low-curvature direction; weakly across edges
  • Feature preservation emerges from anisotropy (no explicit edge detection needed)
Algorithm 5: Anisotropic Smoothing
1: Input: Mesh M, iterations k
2: for vertex v do (κ₁,κ₂,e₁,e₂) ← PrincipalCurvature(v)
3: for vertex v do D[v] ← λ‖·e₁e₁ᵀ + λ⊥·e₂e₂ᵀ
4: for iter = 1..k do V ← V + D·(∇²V)
5: return V

Semantic-Aware Smoothing (Using BraTS Labels)

What We Change

Standard smoothers treat every edge equally. In medical meshes, this can blur boundaries between tumor sub-regions (e.g., core vs edema). We use BraTS segmentation labels to reduce smoothing across label boundaries.

Label-Aware Edge Weighting
$$ w'_{ij} = w_{ij}\,\cdot\,\begin{cases} 1 & \ell_i = \ell_j\\ \alpha & \ell_i \neq \ell_j \end{cases},\quad \alpha = 0.3 $$

Implementation Notes

  • Map voxel labels → mesh vertices (nearest-neighbor lookup)
  • Build adjacency once, then apply boundary scaling to weights
  • Works as a drop-in modifier for Laplacian/Taubin-style smoothers

Takeaway

Semantic-aware weighting preserves clinically meaningful transitions between BraTS sub-regions while still removing Marching Cubes “staircase” artifacts within each region.

When to Use

Use this when you care about sub-region boundaries (e.g., enhancing tumor vs edema) and want to avoid cross-region smoothing artifacts.

Practical Application Guidelines

Volume-Critical Applications

Recommendation: Taubin λ|μ

  • +0.056%±0.047% volume change
  • 89.0%±1.9% smoothing
  • +13.5% aspect ratio improvement
  • Processing: 25ms (avg)
  • Verified on n=20 meshes

Use case: Medical volumetrics, treatment monitoring, longitudinal studies

Visual Quality Priority

Recommendation: Geodesic Heat

  • 97.0%±0.9% smoothness (excellent)
  • +53.4% aspect ratio improvement
  • Respects intrinsic surface geometry
  • 27ms average processing time
  • Heat kernel preserves features

Use case: 3D printing, visualization, surgical planning

Maximum Volume Preservation

Recommendation: Anisotropic

  • -0.022%±0.019% volume (best)
  • 59.5%±1.8% smoothing
  • Feature-preserving diffusion
  • 126ms average processing
  • For precision measurements

Use case: Precision volumetrics, tumor tracking

Important Warnings

Laplacian Volume Loss

Volume loss of -0.92%±0.79% per 10 iterations. Smaller meshes suffer more (up to -3.21%). This compounds with more iterations. Not suitable for volume-sensitive measurements.

Geodesic Heat Volume Loss

Geodesic Heat has similar volume loss (-0.82%±0.71%) to Laplacian. Despite excellent smoothing (97.0%), it's not suitable for precision volumetrics due to shrinkage.

Default Recommendation: Use Taubin λ|μ with λ=0.5, μ=-0.53, 10 iterations for applications requiring both volume accuracy (+0.056%±0.047%) and strong smoothing (89.0%). Verified on n=20 BraTS brain tumor meshes.
